Executive Summary

The Business Story in Two Minutes

Our analysis of 9,994 orders reveals that discount behaviour is the single largest threat to profitability. Currently, 19.4% of all orders are loss-making, leaving the business operating at 64.7% of its profit potential — with $138,515 recoverable through a single policy change. Orders exceeding a 20% discount threshold show a 96.8% loss rate, with average margins collapsing to -108.9%. The West region demonstrates this is solvable — operating at a 10.9% average discount against a company average of 16%, they generate the highest regional profit at $108,418. We recommend implementing a system-enforced 20% discount ceiling company-wide, benchmarked on the West region's governance model. Simultaneously, Tables and Bookcases within Furniture require sub-category specific caps, with discontinuation review if margins remain negative after 180 days.

BQ1 · Category Profitability

Technology Leads. Furniture Bleeds.

Technology generates $145,455 at 15.6% margin. Furniture generates only $18,451 at 3.9% — with Tables alone responsible for -$17,000 in losses.

BQ2 · Discount Impact

The 20% Line Everything Crosses at Its Peril.

Discount explains 74.6% of all profit margin variation. The mathematical break-even is 21.8%. Above 20%, 96.8% of orders are loss-making.

Operational Recommendations

What Should Happen Next

Every recommendation is grounded in a specific finding with a quantified business case.

Priority 1 · Immediate

Implement 20% System-Enforced Discount Cap

Recovers $138,515 in annual profit by preventing 1,348 loss-making orders. Cap enforced at system level with manager-override workflow for exceptions.

Priority 2 · 30 Days

Audit West Region's Discount Governance Model

West operates at 10.9% average discount vs 16% company average. Their policy and approval framework should be documented and mandated company-wide.

Priority 3 · 90 Days

Sub-Category Caps: Tables & Bookcases

Apply sub-category specific 20% cap to Tables (currently 26.1%) and Bookcases (21.1%). Monitor margin performance over 90-day observation window.

Priority 4 · 180 Days

Tables Discontinuation Review

If Tables margin remains below 5% after cap implementation, initiate formal pricing review. If still negative at 180 days, begin discontinuation planning.

Priority 5 · Strategic

Shift Customer Mix Toward Home Office

Home Office generates $407 profit per customer vs $328 for Consumer. Targeted acquisition of Home Office customers improves margin quality without requiring volume growth.

Priority 6 · Strategic

Central Region Demand Audit

Central's average order margin is -10.4% — structurally fragile despite positive total profit. Requires both discount cap enforcement AND demand generation strategy.
